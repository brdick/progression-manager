How to Use and Read

General

You can sort the table using some of the columns. If so, clicking the column name will sort the table by that column.

Some columns are grouped together. Clicking the header of that group of columns will collapse them all to make the table smaller.

Most variable fields can be edited. Click on the field to change the value of it. Press ENTER, TAB, or click anywhere else to finish editing.

Data can be imported from GOOD format. GOOD format comes from programs like Inventory Kamera, which automatically navigate your inventory and scan your screen to read your items. Otherwise, you'd need to fill it out manually as described above.

All data is saved to your browser automatically. This is done using web storage, not cookies, which most modern browsers support. If yours doesn't, get a new one.

Character Page

Marking Favorites

Clicking on the first column allows you to mark a character as a favorite. This will make it easier to see their material requirements on the Materials page, as well as let you sort them above non-favorites.

Phase Column

Green fields indicate that you have all the materials to ascend the character, though some may need to be crafted.

Level Column

Green fields indicate that your character is ready to be ascended, and you have all the materials to do so, though some may need to be crafted.

Yellow fields indicate that your character is not at the maximum level for their phase.

Talent Columns

Uncolored fields indicate that the talent is fully leveled up for your character's phase.

Green fields indicate that you can level up the talent right now, though some materials may need to be crafted.

Yellow fields indicate that you are missing materials needed to level up, but you can farm for them today.

Red fields indicate that you are missing materials needed to level up, specifically Mastery materials, and you can't farm more of them today.

Material Columns

Editing material fields will change the amount of that material in your inventory.

Yellow quantities indicate that you don't have enough of that specific material, but you can craft more to obtain the necessary amount.

Red quantities indicate that you don't have enough of that specific material, and you will need to farm to obtain more of it.
